Network Closet

Located on the lower level of the museum, the network closet contains the two PCs which control the projectors and screens on the fifth floor.

The closet is room number 0741, and can be accessed via the doors next to collections management, or via the doors by security.

Getting into the hallway itself requires a PIN; if you are a Harvard Art Museum employee, speak to security about setting one up.

The larger of the two PCs controls the nine screens, while the narrower controls the two projectors, although other arrangements are occassionally devised based on the needs of the project.

If you are trying to access these PCs, this is the most direct route to do so.

USB ports are available on the front of each PC: in order to access the ones on the projector PC, open the right-hand panel on its front.

The screen PC also has a disk drive, which can be accessed, again, by opening the panel on its front.

If the PCs have been turned off (outside of the usual scheduled cycle), this is the only place from which they can be turned on: simply press the power button on the relevant device(s).
